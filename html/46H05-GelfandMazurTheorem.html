Gelfand-Mazur theorem


Theorem - Let 𝒜 be a unital Banach algebra over ℂ that is also a division algebra (i.e. every non-zero element is invertible). Then 𝒜 is isometrically isomorphic to ℂ.

Proof : Let e denote the unit of 𝒜.

Let x∈𝒜 and σ⁢(x) be its spectrum. It is known that the spectrum is a non-empty set (http://planetmath.org/SpectrumIsANonEmptyCompactSet) in ℂ.

Let λ∈σ⁢(x). Since x-λ⁢e is not invertible and 𝒜 is a division algebra, we must have x-λ⁢e=0 and so x=λ⁢e

Let ϕ:ℂ⟶𝒜 be defined by ϕ⁢(λ)=λ⁢e.

It is clear that ϕ is an injective algebra homomorphism.

By the above discussion, ϕ is also surjective.

It is isometric because ∥λ⁢e∥=|λ|⁢∥e∥=|λ|

Therefore, 𝒜 is isometrically isomorphic to ℂ. □
