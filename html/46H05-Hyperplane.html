hyperplane


Let E be a linear space over a field k. A hyperplane H in E is defined as the set of the form

H={x∈E:f⁢(x)=a}

where a∈k and f is a nonzero linear functional, f:E→k. If k=ℝ or ℂ, then H is called a real hyperplane or complex hyperplane respectively.

Remark. When k=ℂ, the word “hyperplane” also has a more restrictive meaning: it is the zero set of a complex linear functional (by setting a=0 above).
