Riesz-Fischer theorem


Let {en} be an orthonormal basis for a (real or complex) infinite-dimensional Hilbert space ℋ. If {cn} is a sequence of (real or complex) numbers such that ∑|cn|2 converges, then there is an x∈ℋ such that x=∑n=1∞cn⁢en, and cn=⟨x,en⟩.
