proof of von Neumann double commutant theorem


Lemma - Let H be a Hilbert space and B⁢(H) its algebra of bounded operators. Let 𝒩 be a *-subalgebra of B⁢(H) that contains the identity operator and is closed in the strong operator topology. If T∈𝒩′′, the double commutant of 𝒩, then for each x∈H there is an operator A∈𝒩 such that ∥(A-T)⁢x∥<1.

: Let 𝒩⁢x¯⊆H be the closure of the subspace 𝒩⁢x:={S⁢x:S∈𝒩}. It is clear that 𝒩⁢x is an invariant subspace for 𝒩, hence so is its closure 𝒩⁢x¯ (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/InvariantSubspacesForSelfAdjointAlgebrasOfOperators), Proposition 5).

Let P be the orthogonal projection onto 𝒩⁢x¯. Since 𝒩⁢x¯ is invariant for 𝒩, we have that P∈𝒩′ (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/InvariantSubspacesForSelfAdjointAlgebrasOfOperators), last theorem). Since 𝒩 contains the identity operator, we know that x belongs to 𝒩⁢x¯. Hence,

T⁢x=T⁢P⁢x=P⁢T⁢x

where the last equality comes from the fact that T∈𝒩′′ and P∈𝒩′. Thus, we see that T⁢x∈𝒩⁢x¯, which implies that there exists an A∈𝒩 such that ∥T⁢x-A⁢x∥<1. □

(1)⟹(2) Since ℳ′′ is the commutant of some set, namely it is the commutant of ℳ′, it follows that ℳ′′ is closed in the weak operator topology (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/CommutantIsAWeakOperatorClosedSubalgebra)). But we are assuming that ℳ=ℳ′′, hence ℳ is closed in the weak operator topology.

(2)⟹(3) This part is obvious since the weak operator topology is weaker than the strong operator topology.

(3)⟹(1) Suppose ℳ is closed in the strong operator topology.

A subset of B⁢(H) is always contained in its double commutant, thus ℳ⊆ℳ′′. So it remains to prove the opposite inclusion.

Let T∈ℳ′′. We are going to prove that T belongs to the strong operator closure of ℳ, and since ℳ is closed under this topology, it will follow that T∈ℳ.

Recall that the strong operator topology is the topology in B⁢(H) generated by the family of seminorms ∥⋅∥x,x∈H defined by ∥S∥x:=∥S⁢x∥. A local base around T, in this topology, consists of sets of the form

V⁢(x1,…,xn;ϵ):={S∈B⁢(H):∥(S-T)⁢xi∥≤ϵ,i=1,…,n},x1,…,xn∈H,ϵ>0

We can however consider ϵ to be 1, since V⁢(x1,…,xn;ϵ)=V⁢(ϵ-1⁢x1,…,ϵ-1⁢xn;1).

For every x1,…,xn∈H we want to find A∈ℳ such that A∈V⁢(x1,…,xn; 1), i.e. such that ∥(A-T)⁢xi∥<1, for each i.

Let H~ be the direct sum of Hilbert spaces H~:=⊕i=1nH. For every A∈B⁢(H) let A~∈B⁢(H~) be the direct sum of bounded operators (http://planetmath.org/DirectSumOfBoundedOperatorsOnHilbertSpaces) A~:=⊕i=1nA, i.e.

A~⁢(y1,…,yn)=(A⁢y1,…,A⁢yn),y1,…,yn∈H

We have that 𝒩:={A~:A∈ℳ} is a *-algebra of bounded operators in H~.

Claim 1 - T~∈𝒩′′.

The algebra B⁢(H~) can be canonically identified with the algebra of n×n matrices with entries in B⁢(H), and 𝒩 corresponds to the diagonal matrices with an element A∈ℳ in the diagonal. Thus, it is easy to check that 𝒩′ is precisely the set of matrices whose entries belong to ℳ′.

Since the unit matrices (http://planetmath.org/UnitMatrix) belong to 𝒩′, it follows that 𝒩′′ consists solely of diagonal matrices with one element on the diagonal (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/CentralizerOfMatrixUnits)). It is easy to check that 𝒩′′ is precisely the set of diagonal matrices with one element of ℳ′′ in the diagonal. Hence, we conclude that T~∈𝒩′′, and Claim 1 is proved.

Now, we observe that 𝒩 is a *-subalgebra of B⁢(H~) that contains the identity operator. Since ℳ is closed in the strong operator topology, it follows easily that 𝒩 is also closed in the strong operator topology. Since T~∈𝒩′′, Lemma 1 that for each x~:=(x1,…,xn)∈H~ there exists an operator A~∈𝒩 such that ∥(A~-T~)⁢x~∥<1. But this is implies that ∥(A-T)⁢xi∥<1 for each 1≤i≤n.

Thus, T∈V⁢(x1,…,xn; 1). Hence we conclude that T belongs to the operator closure of ℳ, but since ℳ is closed under this topology, T∈ℳ.

We conclude that ℳ′′=ℳ. □
