direct sum of Hilbert spaces


Let {Hi}i∈I be a family of Hilbert spaces indexed by a set I. The direct sum of this family of Hilbert spaces, denoted as

⊕i∈IHi

consists of all elements v of the Cartesian product (http://planetmath.org/GeneralizedCartesianProduct) of {Hi}i∈I such that ∑∥vi∥2<∞. Of course, for the previous sum to be finite only at most a countable number of vi can be non-zero.

Vector addition and scalar multiplication are defined termwise: If u,v∈⊕i∈IHi, then (u+v)i=ui+vi and (s⁢v)i=s⁢vi.

The inner product of two vectors is defined as

⟨u,v⟩=∑i∈I⟨ui,vi⟩
