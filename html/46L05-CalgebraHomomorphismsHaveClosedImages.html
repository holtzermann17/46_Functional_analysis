C*-algebra homomorphisms have closed images


Theorem - Let f:𝒜⟶ℬ be a *-homomorphism between the C*-algebras (http://planetmath.org/CAlgebra) 𝒜 and ℬ. Then f has closed (http://planetmath.org/ClosedSet) image (http://planetmath.org/Function), i.e. f⁢(𝒜) is closed in ℬ.

Thus, the image f⁢(𝒜) is a C*-subalgebra of ℬ.

Proof: The kernel of f, Ker⁢f, is a closed two-sided ideal of 𝒜, since f is continuous (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/HomomorphismsOfCAlgebrasAreContinuous)). Factoring threw the quotient C*-algebra 𝒜/Ker⁢f we obtain an injective *-homomorphism f~:𝒜/Ker⁢f⟶ℬ.

Injective *-homomorphisms between C*-algebras are known to be isometric (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/InjectiveCAlgebraHomomorphismIsIsometric)), hence the image f~⁢(𝒜/Ker⁢f) is closed in ℬ.

Since the images f~⁢(𝒜/Ker⁢f) and f⁢(𝒜) coincide we conclude that f⁢(𝒜) is closed in ℬ. □
