Sobolev inequality


For 1≤p<n, define the Sobolev conjugate of p as

p*:=n⁢pn-p.

Note that -n/p*=1-n/p.

In the following statement ∇ represent the weak derivative and W1,p⁢(Ω) is the Sobolev space of functions u∈Lp⁢(Ω) whose weak derivative ∇⁡u is itself in Lp⁢(Ω).

Theorem 1

Assume that p∈[1,n) and let Ω be a bounded, open subset of Rn with Lipschitz boundary. Then there is a constant C>0 such that, for all u∈W1,p⁢(Ω) one has

∥u∥Lp*⁢(Ω)≤C⁢∥∇⁡u∥Lp⁢(Ω).

We can restate the previous Theorem by saying that the Sobolev space W1,p⁢(Ω) is a subspace of the Lebesgue space Lp*⁢(Ω) and that the inclusion map i:W1,p⁢(Ω)→Lq*⁢(Ω) is continuous.
