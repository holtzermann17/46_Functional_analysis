Uniform Algebra


Definition.

A commutative, unital Banach algebra (𝒜,∥⋅∥) is called uniform Banach algebra (for short: uB algebra) if for all f∈𝒜 we have

∥f2∥ =∥f∥2

In what follows we will show that the Gelfand transform Γ𝒜:𝒜↦𝒜^ of a commutative, unital Banach algebra 𝒜 is an isometry if and only if 𝒜 is a uniform Banach algebra.

Denote by M⁢(𝒜) the space of (continuous) characters on 𝒜. Recall that for all f∈𝒜 the spectrum σ⁢(f) of f is identical with the range f^⁢(M⁢(𝒜)) and the spectral radius r⁢(f)=∥f^∥M⁢(𝒜)=limn→∞⁡∥fn∥1n

Proposition 1. A Banach algebra 𝒜 is uniform if and only if it’s Gelfand transform Γ𝒜:𝒜→𝒜^,f↦f^ is isometric.

Proof.

If f↦f^ is an isometry we have ∥f2∥=∥f^2∥M⁢(𝒜)=∥f^∥M⁢(𝒜)2=∥f∥2.

Conversely assume ∥f2∥=∥f∥2 for all f∈𝒜. Then by induction we have ∥f2n∥=∥f∥2n for all n∈ℕ. Hence ∥f∥=∥f2n∥12n→∥f^∥M⁢(𝒜). ∎

The following characterization is also often given as the definition of a uB algebra.

Proposition 2. A Banach algebra 𝒜 is uniform iff it is topologically and algebraically isomorphic to a closed, pointseparating subalgebra of C⁢(X) for X a compact Hausdorff space.

Proof.

Since 𝒜^ separates the points of the compact, nonempty space M⁢(𝒜) we see that a uB algebra 𝒜 must have this property.

Conversely, let 𝒜 be a closed pointseparating subalgebra of C⁢(X). Then clearly ∥f∥X2=∥f2∥X for all f∈𝒜. ∎
