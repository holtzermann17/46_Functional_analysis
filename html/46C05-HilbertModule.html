Hilbert module


Definition 1.

A (right) pre-Hilbert module over a C*-algebra A is a right A-module ℰ equipped with an A-valued inner product ⟨-,-⟩:ℰ×ℰ→A, i.e. a sesquilinear pairing satisfying

⟨u,v⁢a⟩ = ⟨u,v⟩⁢a (1)
⟨u,v⟩ = ⟨v,u⟩* (2)
⟨v,v⟩ ≥ 0, with ⁢⟨v,v⟩=0⁢ iff ⁢v=0, (3)

for all u,v∈ℰ and a∈A. Note, positive definiteness is well-defined due to the notion of positivity for C*-algebras. The norm of an element v∈ℰ is defined by ∥v∥=∥⟨v,v⟩∥.

Definition 2.

A (right) Hilbert module over a C*-algebra A is a right pre-Hilbert module over A which is complete with respect to the norm.

Example 1 (Hilbert spaces)

A complex Hilbert space is a Hilbert C-module.

Example 2 (C*-algebras)

A C*-algebra A is a Hilbert A-module with inner product ⟨a,b⟩=a*⁢b.

Definition 3.

A Hilbert A-B-bimodule is a (right) Hilbert module ℰ over a C*-algebra B together with a *-homomorphism π from a C*-algebra A to End(ℰ).
