every finite dimensional normed vector space is a Banach space


Theorem 1.

Every finite dimensional normed vector space is a Banach space.

Proof. Suppose (V,∥⋅∥) is the normed vector space, and (ei)i=1N is a basis for V. For x=∑j=1Nλj⁢ej, we can then define

∥x∥′=∑j=1N|λj|2

whence ∥⋅∥′:V→ℝ is a norm for V. Since all norms on a finite dimensional vector space are equivalent (http://planetmath.org/ProofThatAllNormsOnFiniteVectorSpaceAreEquivalent), there is a constant C>0 such that

1C⁢∥x∥′≤∥x∥≤C⁢∥x∥′,x∈V.

To prove that V is a Banach space, let x1,x2,… be a Cauchy sequence in (V,∥⋅∥). That is, for all ε>0 there is an M≥1 such that

∥xj-xk∥<ε,for all⁢j,k≥M.

Let us write each xk in this sequence in the basis (ej) as xk=∑j=1Nλk,j⁢ej for some constants λk,j∈ℂ. For k,l≥1 we then have

∥xk-xl∥ ≥ 1C⁢∥xk-xl∥′
≥ 1C⁢∑j=1N|λk,j-λl,j|2
≥ 1C⁢|λk,j-λl,j|

for all j=1,…,N. It follows that (λk,1)k=1∞,…,(λk,N)k=1∞ are Cauchy sequences in ℂ. As ℂ is complete, these converge to some complex numbers λ1,…,λN. Let x=∑j=1Nλj⁢ej.

For each k=1,2,…, we then have

∥x-xk∥ ≤ C⁢∥x-xk∥′
≤ C⁢∑j=1N|λj-λk,j|2.

By taking k→∞ it follows that (xj) converges to x∈V. □
