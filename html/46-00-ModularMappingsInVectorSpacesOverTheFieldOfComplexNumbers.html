modular mappings in vector spaces over the field of complex numbers


Suppose X is a ℂ-vector space. A mapping ρ:X→[0,∞] is called modular if the following three conditions are satisfied:

  1. 1.

    ρ⁢(x)=0 if and only if x=0.

  2. 2.

    ρ⁢(α⁢x)=ρ⁢(x) for all x∈X and for all scalars α such that |α|=1.

  3. 3.

    ρ⁢(α⁢x+β⁢y)≤ρ⁢(x)+ρ⁢(y) for all x,y∈X and for all scalars α and β such that α+β=1 and α,β≥0.
