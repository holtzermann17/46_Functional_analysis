Banach algebra


Definition 1.

A Banach algebra 𝒜 is a Banach space (over ℂ) with an multiplication law compatible with the norm which turns 𝒜 into an algebra. Compatibility with the norm means that, for all a,b∈𝒜, it is the case that the following product inequality holds:

∥a⁢b∥≤∥a∥⁢∥b∥
Definition 2.

A Banach *-algebra is a Banach algebra 𝒜 with a map :*𝒜→𝒜 which satisfies the following properties:

a** = a, (1)
(a⁢b)* = b*⁢a*, (2)
(a+b)* = a*+b*, (3)
(λ⁢a)* = λ¯⁢a* ∀λ∈ℂ, (4)
∥a*∥ = ∥a∥, (5)

where λ¯ is the complex conjugation of λ. In other words, the operator * is an involution.

Example 1

The algebra of bounded operators on a Banach space is a Banach algebra for the operator norm.
