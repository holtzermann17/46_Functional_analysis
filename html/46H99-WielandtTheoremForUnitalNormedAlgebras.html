Wielandt theorem for unital normed algebras


Theorem. (Wielandt (1949)) Let A be a normed unital algebra (with unit e). If x,y∈A then x⁢y-y⁢x≠e.

Proof.

Assume there are x,y∈A such that x⁢y-y⁢x=e. Then for all n∈ℕ we have

xn⁢y-y⁢xn =n⁢xn-1≠0

We prove this by induction over n∈ℕ. It holds for n=1 by assumption. Assume it is valid for n∈ℕ. Then xn≠0 and

xn+1⁢y-y⁢xn+1 =xn⁢(x⁢y-y⁢x)+(xn⁢y-y⁢xn)⁢x
=xn⁢e+n⁢xn-1⁢x=xn⁢e+n⁢xn=(n+e)⁢xn

From this identity it follows that

n⁢∥xn-1∥ =∥xn⁢y-y⁢xn∥≤2⁢∥xn∥⁢∥y∥≤2⁢∥xn-1∥⁢∥x∥⁢∥y∥

It follows that n≤2⁢||x||⁢||y|| for all n∈ℕ which is impossible. ∎

Corollary. The identity operator on a Hilbert space ℋ cannot be expressed as a commutator of two bounded linear operators in ℒ⁢(ℋ).

Remark. The above can be understood as a version of the uncertainty principle in one dimension. Let H=L2⁢(ℝ). Let q:H→H be q⁢(f)⁢(x):=x⁢f⁢(x) with D⁢(q)={f∈L2⁢(ℝ):x↦x⁢f⁢(x)∈L2⁢(ℝ)}, the coordinate operator and p:H→H,p⁢(f)⁢(x)=-i⁢f′⁢(x) the momentum operator with D⁢(p):={f∈L2⁢(ℝ):f⁢absolutely  continuous,f′∈L2⁢(ℝ)}. It follows that

p⁢q-q⁢p =-i⁢idD⁢on⁢D=D⁢(q)∩D⁢(p)

According to the corollary D=L2⁢(ℝ) can never be the case.
