proof of quotients in C*-algebras


Proof: We have that ℐ is self-adjoint (http://planetmath.org/InvolutaryRing), since it is a closed ideal of a C*-algebra (http://planetmath.org/CAlgebra) (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/ClosedIdealsInCAlgebrasAreSelfAdjoint)). Hence, the involution in 𝒜 induces a well-defined involution in 𝒜/ℐ by (x+ℐ)*:=x*+ℐ.

Recall that, since ℐ is closed, the quotient norm is indeed a norm in 𝒜/ℐ that makes 𝒜/ℐ a Banach algebra (see this entry (http://planetmath.org/QuotientsOfBanachAlgebras)). Thus we only have to prove the C* to prove that 𝒜/ℐ is a C*-algebra.

Recall that C*-algebras have approximate identities (http://planetmath.org/CAlgebrasHaveApproximateIdentities). Notice that ℐ itself is a C*-algebra and pick an approximate identity (eλ) in ℐ such that

  • •

    each eλ is positive.

  • •

    ∥eλ∥≤1

We will only prove the case when 𝒜 has an identity element e. For the non-unital case, one can consider 𝒜 as a C*-subalgebra of its minimal unitization and the same proof will still work.

Let ∥⋅∥q denote the quotient norm in 𝒜/ℐ. We claim that for every x∈𝒜:

∥x+ℐ∥q=limλ⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥ (1)

We will prove the above equality as a lemma at the end of the entry. Assuming this result, it follows that for every a∈𝒜

∥x+ℐ∥q2=lim⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥2=lim⁡∥(e-eλ)⁢x*⁢x⁢(e-eλ)∥≤lim⁡∥(e-eλ)∥⁢∥x*⁢x⁢(e-eλ)∥

Since each eλ is positive and ∥eλ∥≤1 we know that its spectrum lies on the interval [0,1]. Hence e-eλ is also positive and its spectrum also lies on the interval [0,1]. Thus, ∥e-eλ∥≤1. Therefore:

∥x+ℐ∥q2≤lim⁡∥(e-eλ)∥⁢∥x*⁢x⁢(e-eλ)∥≤lim⁡∥x*⁢x⁢(e-eλ)∥=∥x*⁢x+ℐ∥q

Since 𝒜/ℐ is a Banach algebra, we also have ∥x*⁢x+ℐ∥q≤∥x+ℐ∥q2 and so

∥x+ℐ∥q2=∥x*⁢x+ℐ∥q

which proves that 𝒜/ℐ is a C*-algebra. □

We now prove equality (1) as a lemma.

Lemma - Suppose 𝒜 is a C*-algebra with identity element e. Let ℐ⊂𝒜 be a closed ideal and (eλ) be an approximate identity in ℐ such that each eλ is positive and ∥eλ∥≤1. Then

∥x+ℐ∥q=limλ⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥

for every x in 𝒜.

Proof: Since y⁢(e-eλ)⟶0 for every y∈ℐ it follows that

lim sup⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥ = lim sup⁡∥x-x⁢eλ-y+y⁢eλ∥
= lim sup⁡∥(x-y)⁢(e-eλ)∥
≤ ∥x-y∥

Therefore, taking the infimum over all y∈ℐ we obtain:

lim sup⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥≤infy∈ℐ⁡∥x-y∥=∥x+ℐ∥q

Also, since x⁢eλ∈ℐ,

lim inf⁡∥x⁢(e-eλ)∥≥infy∈ℐ⁡∥x-y∥=∥x+ℐ∥q

and this proves the lemma. □
