proof of Banach-Alaoglu theorem


For any x∈X, let Dx={z∈ℂ:|z|≤∥x∥} and D=Πx∈X⁢Dx. Since Dx is a compact subset of ℂ, D is compact in product topology by Tychonoff theorem.

We prove the theorem by finding a homeomorphism that maps the closed unit ball BX* of X* onto a closed subset of D. Define Φx:BX*→Dx by Φx⁢(f)=f⁢(x) and Φ:BX*→D by Φ=Πx∈X⁢Φx, so that Φ⁢(f)=(f⁢(x))x∈X. Obviously, Φ is one-to-one, and a net (fα) in BX* converges to f in weak-* topology of X* iff Φ⁢(fα) converges to Φ⁢(f) in product topology, therefore Φ is continuous and so is its inverse Φ-1:Φ⁢(BX*)→BX*.

It remains to show that Φ⁢(BX*) is closed. If (Φ⁢(fα)) is a net in Φ⁢(BX*), converging to a point d=(dx)x∈X∈D, we can define a function f:X→ℂ by f⁢(x)=dx. As limα⁡Φ⁢(fα⁢(x))=dx for all x∈X by definition of weak-* convergence, one can easily see that f is a linear functional in BX* and that Φ⁢(f)=d. This shows that d is actually in Φ⁢(BX*) and finishes the proof.
