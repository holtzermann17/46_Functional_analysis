property of uniformly convex Banach Space


Theorem 1.

Let X be a uniformly convex Banach space. Let (xn) be a sequence in X such that lim⁡xn=x in the weak-topology (w⁢(X,X*)) and lim sup⁡∥xn∥≤∥x∥.Then xn converges to x.

Proof.

For x=0 the claim is obvious, so suppose that x≠0.The sequence (xn)n≥1 converges to x for w-t⁢o⁢p⁢o⁢l⁢o⁢g⁢y ⇒∥x∥≤lim inf⁡∥xn∥.So let λn=max⁡{∥x∥,∥xn∥} and we have that lim⁡λn=∥x∥. Define yn=xnλn and y=x∥x∥.Then yn converges to y in w-t⁢o⁢p⁢o⁢l⁢o⁢g⁢y. We conclude that ∥y∥≤lim inf⁡∥yn+y2∥. Also, ∥y∥=1,∥yn∥≤1 so we have that lim⁡∥yn+y2∥=1. As the Banach space is uniformly convex one can easily see that lim⁡∥yn-y∥=0. Therefore xn converges to x. The proof is complete. ∎
