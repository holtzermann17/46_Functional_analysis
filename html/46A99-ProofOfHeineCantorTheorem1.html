proof of Heine-Cantor theorem


We prove this theorem in the case when X and Y are metric spaces.

Suppose f is not uniformly continuous. Then

∃ϵ>0⁢∀δ>0⁢∃x,y∈X d⁢(x,y)<δ⁢but⁢d⁢(f⁢(x),f⁢(y))≥ϵ.

In particular by letting δ=1/k we can construct two sequences xk and yk such that

d(xk,yk)<1/kandd(f(xk),f(yk)≥ϵ.

Since X is compact the two sequence have convergent subsequences i.e.

xkj→x¯∈X,ykj→y¯∈X.

Since d⁢(xk,yk)→0 we have x¯=y¯. Being f continuous we hence conclude d⁢(f⁢(xkj),f⁢(ykj))→0 which is a contradiction being d⁢(f⁢(xk),f⁢(yk))≥ϵ.
