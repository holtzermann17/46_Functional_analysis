uniformly convex space


A normed space is uniformly convex iff ∀ϵ>0 there exists δ>0 that satisfies for ∥x∥≤1 ∥y∥≤1 and ∥x-y∥>ϵ ⇒ ∥x+y2∥≤1-δ.

For example it is easily seen that the normed space (ℝ2,∥.∥2) is uniformly convex space. Also Lp and lp spaces for 1<p<∞ are uniformly convex, see J.A. Clarkson, ”Uniformly convex spaces”, Trans. Amer. Math. Society, 40 (1936), 396-414.
