\begin{definition}
A finite quantum group $Q_{Gf}$ is a pair $(\mathbb{H},\Phi)$ of a finite-dimensional 
$C^*$-algebra $\mathbb{H}$ with a comultiplication $ \Phi$ such that $(\mathbb{H},\Phi)$ is a Hopf  $^*$-algebra.
\end{definition}

 
 Note that one obtains the dual Hopf algebra of a commutative, finite quantum group via
Fourier transformation of the group's elements.
